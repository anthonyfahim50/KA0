About CASA

Standing up for children who need a voice

Court Appointed Special Advocates (CASA) are trained community volunteers appointed by judges to advocate for the best interests of children who have experienced abuse or neglect. A CASA volunteer gets to know the child, speaks for them in court, and works to ensure they don't slip through the cracks of an overburdened system.

For many children, a CASA volunteer is the one steady, caring adult in their life during a frightening and uncertain time — and research shows children with a CASA are more likely to find safe, permanent homes and to succeed in school.

That work is only possible because of supporters like Kappa Alpha Theta. Since adopting CASA as its national philanthropy in 1989, Theta chapters across North America have rallied behind these children every single year.
