About the Chapter

Our story, our standards

Founded in 1870 on the belief that women deserve the widest opportunity for intellectual and personal growth, Kappa Alpha Theta has built leaders and lifelong sisters — and Eta Omega carries that legacy at Saint Louis University.

Our Mission

A commitment to personal excellence — and to each other

Kappa Alpha Theta offers a lifelong opportunity for intellectual and personal growth and leadership development through a commitment to personal excellence. We are not a club to be joined but a sisterhood to be lived — one that holds each member to a higher standard on campus, in the community, and beyond.

At Eta Omega, that mission shows up in our scholarship, our service to CASA and St. Louis, the leaders we develop, and the sisters who stay connected for a lifetime.

Our History

1870

Theta is founded

On January 27, 1870, Bettie Locke and three founders established Kappa Alpha Theta at Indiana Asbury (now DePauw University) — the first Greek-letter fraternity known among women.

1989

A national commitment to CASA

Theta adopts Court Appointed Special Advocates (CASA) as its national philanthropy, standing for children who have experienced abuse or neglect.
